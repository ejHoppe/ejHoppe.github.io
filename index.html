ERIC HOPPE

Application, Software, & Web Development

After graduating from high school, I lacked direction. I had no idea what I wanted to do, but I did have an urge to serve, so I enlisted into the United States Air Force. Over the span of seven years, I served, first as an Airborne Linguist and later as a Signals Analyst where I served two tours in Afghanistan.

Once I separated from the Air Force, I transitioned to military consulting with Booz Allen Hamilton. While consulting for the firm, I was introduced to Python, and it was then that I discovered I had a passion for programming. Eventually, my project transitioned from California to Oklahoma, so I decided to move back to Oregon to finally pursue a college education.

After three years, I just graduated from Willamette University with a major in computer science and a minor in mathematics. I graduated with department honors for my senior thesis on Generative Adversarial Networks.
